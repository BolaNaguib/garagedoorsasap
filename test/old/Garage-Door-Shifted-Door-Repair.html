Why Do Garage Door Springs Break?

The life of a garage door spring is based on cycles. The minimum specification is 10,000 cycles. That means the garage door will go up and down on average 10,000 times before the spring breaks. The more you use your garage door, the sooner the spring will break, and need to be replaced.

Springs that Suit Your Garage Door Best!

Garage Door Springs come in different shapes and sizes, Based on the weight of your Door and the Length of the spring, if the wrong spring is installed, it would shorten the life span of the Garage Door and the Garage Door Opener and it would be more likely that the new springs will break shortly after the replacement.

Garage Door Springs That Last!

Why is everyone telling you to change two springs while only one is broken?

It’s commonly known that if one spring broke it would be adding double the pressure on the other one, that makes it most like to break really soon, If you choose to change the broke one only, most likely the second one will break shortly after the replacement,

Is it better to replace the Garage Door Rollers when you replace Garage Door Springs?

Garage door rollers play an important role in the garage door ecosystem. When they are running properly, your garage door will operate more efficiently. The life expectancy of most garage door rollers out there is around 10,000 cycles That’s why it is recommended that you get your garage door rollers replaced while replacing your springs, If you choose not to change them, it will have a negative effect on your garage door opener, adding higher pressure on it, which decreases its life expectancy.

Why you should never try to replace your own Garage Door Springs?

Safety First!

Replacing garage door springs is dangerous because the springs are under tension. If you do not use the right tools and follow safe procedures, you could seriously harm yourself, a member of your family or your property in the process,
